I am a PhD student in Social Statistics at the University of Manchester and Research Assistant at the University of Oxford. My main research interests revolve around Social Network Analysis and Computational Social Science. I aspire to apply advanced statistical methods to real-world problems and have versatile experience in working with time-series, spatial, textual and network data from different domains.

Before coming to Manchester, I worked as a consultant at the World Bank. My projects at the World Bank involved the usage of novel data sources for education research. I exploited the Big Data of CVs posted on HeadHunter website for estimating the supply of skills in the labour market and utilised linked data from financial and administrative sources to inform policy decision-making processes.

I am also an active participant in Data Analytics competitions hosted on Kaggle and other platforms. Most recently, Ekaterina Melianova and I took 1st place in DS4G - EIE competition organised by The Environmental Insights Explorer team at Google and became prize-winners in CDP - Unlocking Climate Solutions competition and COVID-19 Facebook challenge implemented in partnership with Facebook Data For Good.

During my work and studies, I used a variety of programming languages such as Python, R, and JavaScript. I also have experience with database management using SQL, MongoDB, and PySpark.

More detailed information can be found in my CV.
